## Part 5: Common Mistakes in QM Qualifying Exams

### Avoid These Pitfalls

**Conceptual Traps:**
- Forgetting that $[\hat{A}, \hat{B}] \neq 0$ implies uncertainty
- Confusing eigenstates of different operators
- Misapplying perturbation theory to wrong regime
- Forgetting symmetry constraints for identical particles

**Procedural Traps:**
- Using non-degenerate PT for degenerate states
- Wrong order of operations in angular momentum addition
- Forgetting rotating wave approximation
- Incorrect matching conditions at boundaries

**Algebraic Traps:**
- Sign errors in commutators
- Factor of 2 errors with spin-1/2
- Forgetting normalization constants
- Errors in Gaussian integrals

**Time Management Traps:**
- Spending too long on one problem
- Not attempting all problems
- Not checking answers
- Doing more than asked
