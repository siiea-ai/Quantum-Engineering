## Part A: Pauli Group

### Solution 1

**(a)** $$(X_1Z_2)(Y_1X_2)$$

$$= (X_1Y_1)(Z_2X_2) = (iZ_1)(iY_2) = i^2 Z_1Y_2 = -Z_1Y_2$$

**(b)** $$(X_1X_2X_3)(Z_1Z_2Z_3)$$

$$= (X_1Z_1)(X_2Z_2)(X_3Z_3) = (-iY_1)(-iY_2)(-iY_3) = -i^3 Y_1Y_2Y_3 = iY_1Y_2Y_3$$

**(c)** $$(iY_1Z_2)(-iX_1Y_2)$$

$$= (i)(-i)(Y_1X_1)(Z_2Y_2) = (1)(-iZ_1)(iX_2) = -i^2 Z_1X_2 = Z_1X_2$$

**(d)** $$(X_1Z_2Y_3)^2$$

$$= X_1^2 Z_2^2 Y_3^2 = I \cdot I \cdot I = I$$

### Solution 2

**(a)** $$X_1X_2$$ and $$Z_1Z_2$$:

Check positions: $$X_1$$ anticommutes with $$Z_1$$, $$X_2$$ anticommutes with $$Z_2$$.
Two anticommutations $$\Rightarrow$$ overall sign: $$(-1)^2 = +1$$ $$\Rightarrow$$ **Commute**

**(b)** $$X_1Z_2$$ and $$Z_1X_2$$:

Position 1: $$X_1$$ vs $$Z_1$$ anticommute
Position 2: $$Z_2$$ vs $$X_2$$ anticommute
Two anticommutations $$\Rightarrow$$ **Commute**

**(c)** $$Y_1Y_2Y_3$$ and $$X_1X_2X_3$$:

Each $$Y_i$$ anticommutes with $$X_i$$ (since $$YX = -XY$$).
Three anticommutations $$\Rightarrow (-1)^3 = -1$$ $$\Rightarrow$$ **Anticommute**

**(d)** $$X_1X_2Z_3Z_4$$ and $$Z_1Z_2X_3X_4$$:

Position 1: $$X_1$$ vs $$Z_1$$ anticommute
Position 2: $$X_2$$ vs $$Z_2$$ anticommute
Position 3: $$Z_3$$ vs $$X_3$$ anticommute
Position 4: $$Z_4$$ vs $$X_4$$ anticommute
Four anticommutations $$\Rightarrow (-1)^4 = +1$$ $$\Rightarrow$$ **Commute**

### Solution 3

**(a)** An element $$P \in Z(\mathcal{G}_n)$$ must commute with all elements of $$\mathcal{G}_n$$.

Consider $$X_1$$ and $$Z_1$$. Any $$P$$ with non-trivial action on qubit 1 will anticommute with either $$X_1$$ or $$Z_1$$.

Therefore $$P$$ must act as identity on all qubits: $$P = \alpha I$$ where $$\alpha \in \{\pm 1, \pm i\}$$.

Thus $$Z(\mathcal{G}_n) = \{\pm I, \pm iI\}$$.

**(b)** $$|\mathcal{G}_n| = 4^{n+1}$$ and $$|Z(\mathcal{G}_n)| = 4$$.

$$|\mathcal{G}_n/Z(\mathcal{G}_n)| = 4^{n+1}/4 = 4^n$$

**(c)** The quotient $$\mathcal{G}_n/Z(\mathcal{G}_n)$$ is generated by the images of $$X_1, Z_1, \ldots, X_n, Z_n$$.

Each generator has order 2 in the quotient (since $$X_i^2 = I \in Z$$).

The generators satisfy no relations except commutativity in the quotient (anticommutation gives a phase in $$Z$$).

Thus $$\mathcal{G}_n/Z(\mathcal{G}_n) \cong \mathbb{Z}_2^{2n}$$.

### Solution 4

**(a)** Two Paulis $$P = X^aZ^b$$ and $$Q = X^{a'}Z^{b'}$$ satisfy:

$$PQ = (-1)^{a \cdot b' + a' \cdot b} QP$$

So $$[P, Q] = 0$$ iff $$a \cdot b' + a' \cdot b \equiv 0 \pmod 2$$.

This is precisely $$\langle (a|b), (a'|b') \rangle = 0$$.

**(b)** For $$(1,0|0,1) = X_1Z_2$$, we need $$(a_1, a_2|b_1, b_2)$$ with:
$$1 \cdot b_1 + a_1 \cdot 0 + 0 \cdot b_2 + a_2 \cdot 1 = b_1 + a_2 \equiv 0$$

Solutions: $$b_1 = a_2$$. There are $$2^3 = 8$$ solutions:
$$(a_1, a_2|a_2, b_2)$$ for $$a_1, a_2, b_2 \in \{0, 1\}$$.

These are: $$I, X_1, Z_2, X_1Z_2, X_2Z_1, X_1X_2Z_1, X_2Z_1Z_2, X_1X_2Z_1Z_2$$.

**(c)** A maximal commuting subset corresponds to a maximal isotropic subspace of $$\mathbb{Z}_2^{2n}$$ under the symplectic form. Such subspaces have dimension $$n$$, hence size $$2^n$$.

### Solution 5

**(a)** The weight enumerator is:
$$W_\mathcal{S}(x, y) = \sum_{S \in \mathcal{S}} x^{n-\text{wt}(S)} y^{\text{wt}(S)}$$

**(b)** For $$[[5,1,3]]$$ code, $$|\mathcal{S}| = 2^4 = 16$$.

The stabilizer contains:
- 1 identity (weight 0)
- 0 weight-1 or weight-2 elements (distance 3)
- Weight-4 elements from products of generators
- Weight-5 elements

Counting carefully (the generators have weight 4):
$$W_\mathcal{S}(x, y) = x^5 + 15x y^4$$

(1 identity, 15 weight-4 operators)

**(c)** The distance is the minimum weight of $$C(\mathcal{S}) \setminus \mathcal{S}$$. If the stabilizer has no low-weight elements, but the centralizer does, those give the distance.

$$X|-\rangle = -|-\rangle$$, so $$-X|-\rangle = |-\rangle$$

Stabilizer: $$\langle -X \rangle = \{I, -X\}$$

Stabilizer: $$\langle X_1X_2, -Z_1Z_2 \rangle$$

**(d)** GHZ state $$(|000\rangle + |111\rangle)/\sqrt{2}$$:

$$Z_2Z_3|\text{GHZ}\rangle = |\text{GHZ}\rangle$$ ✓

Stabilizer: $$\langle X_1X_2X_3, Z_1Z_2, Z_2Z_3 \rangle$$

### Solution 8

Suppose $$S_1, S_2 \in \mathcal{S}$$ both stabilize $$|\psi\rangle$$:
$$S_1|\psi\rangle = |\psi\rangle, \quad S_2|\psi\rangle = |\psi\rangle$$

Then:
$$S_1S_2|\psi\rangle = S_1(S_2|\psi\rangle) = S_1|\psi\rangle = |\psi\rangle$$
$$S_2S_1|\psi\rangle = S_2(S_1|\psi\rangle) = S_2|\psi\rangle = |\psi\rangle$$

If $$S_1S_2 = -S_2S_1$$, then:
$$S_1S_2|\psi\rangle = |\psi\rangle$$ and $$-S_2S_1|\psi\rangle = -|\psi\rangle$$

But $$S_1S_2 = -S_2S_1$$ implies $$|\psi\rangle = -|\psi\rangle$$, so $$|\psi\rangle = 0$$.

Contradiction for non-zero states. Thus stabilizers must commute.

### Solution 9

**(a)** $$|+\rangle^{\otimes n}$$ satisfies $$X_i|+\rangle^{\otimes n} = |+\rangle^{\otimes n}$$ for each $$i$$ since $$X|+\rangle = |+\rangle$$.

The $$n$$ generators $$X_1, \ldots, X_n$$ are independent and commuting.

**(b)** After $$CZ_{12}$$:

$$CZ_{12} X_1 CZ_{12}^\dagger = X_1 Z_2$$ (since CZ adds a Z on qubit 2 when qubit 1 is in $$|1\rangle$$)
$$CZ_{12} X_2 CZ_{12}^\dagger = Z_1 X_2$$

New stabilizers: $$\langle X_1Z_2, Z_1X_2, X_3, \ldots, X_n \rangle$$

**(c)** For graph state $$|G\rangle$$:
- Start with $$X_v$$ stabilizers for each vertex
- Each edge $$(u,v)$$ transforms $$X_u \to X_u Z_v$$ and $$X_v \to Z_u X_v$$

Final stabilizer: $$g_v = X_v \prod_{u \in N(v)} Z_u$$

### Solution 10

**(a)** An n-qubit stabilizer state is determined by $$n$$ generators, each specified by $$2n$$ bits (X and Z components) plus 2 bits for phase.

But generators are not unique—related by $$GL(n, \mathbb{F}_2)$$ transformations.

The count of stabilizer states is:
$$|\text{Stab}_n| = 2^n \prod_{k=0}^{n-1} (2^{n-k} + 1)$$

For large $$n$$: $$|\text{Stab}_n| \approx 2^{n^2 + 2n}$$

**(b)** The space of pure states is a complex projective space of dimension $$2^n - 1$$.

**(c)** For $$n = 10$$:

Stabilizer states: $$\approx 2^{120}$$
All pure states: continuous (uncountable)

As a fraction of Haar-random states, stabilizer states have measure zero.

## Part C: Stabilizer Codes

### Solution 11

**(a)** $$Z_1Z_2$$ and $$Z_2Z_3$$:

Both are products of Z operators, which always commute. ✓

**(b)** Code space: simultaneous $$+1$$ eigenspace.

Basis states must satisfy:
- $$Z_1Z_2 = +1$$: qubits 1,2 have same parity
- $$Z_2Z_3 = +1$$: qubits 2,3 have same parity

Solutions: $$|000\rangle$$ and $$|111\rangle$$.

Code space basis: $$|0_L\rangle = |000\rangle$$, $$|1_L\rangle = |111\rangle$$

**(c)** Logical operators must commute with stabilizers but not be in stabilizer.

$$\overline{X} = X_1X_2X_3$$ (flips all bits, commutes with $$Z_iZ_j$$)
$$\overline{Z} = Z_1$$ (or $$Z_2$$ or $$Z_3$$, all equivalent mod stabilizer)

**(d)** Distance = minimum weight of logical operator = $$\text{wt}(Z_1) = 1$$.

So $$d = 1$$ for Z-type, but $$d = 3$$ for X-type. Overall $$d = 1$$.

Actually for the bit-flip code: $$[[3, 1, 1]]$$ against phase errors, $$[[3, 1, 3]]$$ against bit-flip errors.

**(b)** $$C^\perp = $$ vectors orthogonal to all codewords.

$$\langle (1,1,1), v \rangle = 0$$ for all $$v \in C^\perp$$.

$$C^\perp = \{000, 110, 011, 101\}$$ = even weight vectors.

Parameters: $$[3, 2, 2]$$

**(c)** $$CSS(C, C^\perp)$$: need $$C^\perp \subset C$$? Check: $$110 \notin \{000, 111\}$$.

Actually we need $$C_2 \subset C_1$$. Here $$C_1 = C = \{000, 111\}$$ and we want $$C_2 \subset C$$.

If $$C_2 = \{000\}$$ (trivial), then CSS gives $$[[3, 1 - 0, ?]]$$.

This is just the repetition code as a quantum code: $$[[3, 1, ?]]$$ correcting X errors.

### Solution 19

**(a)** X-stabilizers: $$X^c$$ for $$c \in C_2^\perp$$.
Z-stabilizers: $$Z^h$$ for rows $$h$$ of $$H_1$$.

For $$X^c$$ and $$Z^h$$ to commute:
$$X^c Z^h = (-1)^{c \cdot h} Z^h X^c$$

Need $$c \cdot h = 0$$. Since $$c \in C_2^\perp$$ and $$h$$ is a row of $$H_1$$ (parity check of $$C_1$$), and $$C_2^\perp \subset C_1$$, we have $$H_1 c^T = 0$$, meaning $$c \cdot h = 0$$ for all rows $$h$$.

**(b)** $$\overline{X}$$ = any $$X^v$$ where $$v \in C_1 \setminus C_2^\perp$$ (coset representative).
$$\overline{Z}$$ = any $$Z^w$$ where $$w \in C_2 \setminus C_1^\perp$$.

**(c)** Z errors are detected by X-stabilizers, which use $$C_2^\perp$$. Detection capability = distance of $$C_2^\perp = d_2^\perp$$.

X errors are detected by Z-stabilizers, which use $$C_1$$. Detection = $$d_1$$.

Overall: $$d = \min(d_1, d_2^\perp)$$.

## Part E: Gottesman-Knill Theorem

### Solution 23

**(a)** $$HXH^\dagger$$:
$$H = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{pmatrix}$$

$$HXH^\dagger = \frac{1}{2}\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{pmatrix} = Z$$ ✓

**(b)** $$SXS^\dagger$$:
$$S = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & i \end{pmatrix}$$

$$SXS^\dagger = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & i \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -i \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -i \\ i & 0 \end{pmatrix} = Y$$ ✓

**(c)** CNOT:
$$CNOT \cdot X_1 \cdot CNOT^\dagger = X_1 X_2$$
$$CNOT \cdot Z_2 \cdot CNOT^\dagger = Z_1 Z_2$$

### Solution 27

**(a)** **Algorithm:**
1. Initialize tableau with $$n$$ stabilizers $$Z_1, \ldots, Z_n$$
2. For each gate, update tableau using rules
3. For measurement on qubit $$k$$, check if $$Z_k$$ commutes with all stabilizers

**(b)** **Complexity:**
- Tableau has $$O(n^2)$$ bits
- Each gate update: $$O(n)$$ operations (update one column)
- Total for $$m$$ gates: $$O(n^2 + nm)$$

With destabilizers for measurement: $$O(n^2 m)$$

**(c)** **Measurement simulation:**
- If $$Z_k$$ commutes with all stabilizers: outcome is deterministic (0 or 1 based on whether $$Z_k \in \mathcal{S}$$)
- If $$Z_k$$ anticommutes with some stabilizer $$g_i$$: outcome is random 50/50, and we update $$g_i \to Z_k$$

**(d)** **Why T breaks simulation:**
$$T X T^\dagger = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(X + Y) \notin \mathcal{G}_n$$

T gates don't preserve the Pauli group, so stabilizers become non-Pauli operators, requiring exponential representation.
