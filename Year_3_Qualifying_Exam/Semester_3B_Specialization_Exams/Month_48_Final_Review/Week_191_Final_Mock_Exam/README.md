## Examination Guidelines

### Written Exam Rules

1. **Time Management**
   - Read all problems first (10 minutes)
   - Allocate time based on points
   - Leave time for review (15-20 minutes)
   - Don't get stuck on one problem

2. **Problem Approach**
   - Read carefully, identify what's asked
   - Write given information
   - State relevant equations
   - Show all steps
   - Box final answers
   - Check units and limiting cases

3. **Partial Credit Strategy**
   - Write something for every problem
   - State the approach even if you can't complete
   - Show setup even without final answer
   - Check for dimensional consistency

4. **Formula Sheet Guidelines**
   - One page, both sides
   - Handwritten recommended
   - Key equations only
   - Organized by topic
   - No full solutions or worked examples

### Oral Exam Guidelines

1. **Presentation Phase**
   - Practice to exactly 20 minutes
   - Clear slides/board work
   - Know potential questions
   - Show enthusiasm for research

2. **Question Response**
   - Listen carefully to question
   - Take moment to think
   - Structure your answer
   - Use examples
   - Admit uncertainty gracefully
   - Ask for clarification if needed

3. **Deep Dive Preparation**
   - Know your proposal deeply
   - Anticipate challenging questions
   - Prepare to defend choices
   - Know limitations of your approach

## Scoring Rubrics

### Written Exam Scoring

Each problem is scored 0-10:

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 10 | Perfect solution, clear presentation |
| 8-9 | Minor errors, solid understanding |
| 6-7 | Correct approach, some errors |
| 4-5 | Partial solution, demonstrates some understanding |
| 2-3 | Relevant attempt, significant errors |
| 0-1 | Little relevant content |

### Oral Exam Scoring

Each phase scored 1-5:

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Excellent: Clear, accurate, insightful |
| 4 | Good: Solid understanding, minor gaps |
| 3 | Adequate: Acceptable but room for improvement |
| 2 | Below expectations: Significant gaps |
| 1 | Inadequate: Major deficiencies |

**Passing:** Average score of 3.5 or higher, no phase below 3

## Psychological Preparation

### Before the Exam

1. **Confidence Building**
   - Review your progress over 3 years
   - Remember: you've done the work
   - Visualize successful completion

2. **Physical Preparation**
   - Good sleep (7-8 hours)
   - Healthy meals
   - Light exercise
   - Avoid last-minute cramming

3. **Logistics**
   - Prepare all materials day before
   - Know the exam location/setup
   - Plan arrival time
   - Have backup plans

### During the Exam

1. **Managing Anxiety**
   - Deep breaths before starting
   - Focus on one problem at a time
   - Don't panic if one problem is hard
   - Trust your preparation

2. **Maintaining Focus**
   - Stay hydrated
   - Short mental breaks between problems
   - Keep moving forward
   - Don't dwell on mistakes

### After the Exam

1. **Regardless of Outcome**
   - You've accomplished something significant
   - Learning continues
   - This is one milestone, not the end
   - Rest and recover
