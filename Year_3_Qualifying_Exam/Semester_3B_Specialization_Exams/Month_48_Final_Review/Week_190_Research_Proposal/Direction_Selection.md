## Part 2: Research Landscape Analysis

### 2.1 Current Research Frontiers (2026)

#### Quantum Computing Hardware

**Superconducting Qubits**
- Current leaders: IBM, Google, Rigetti, IQM
- Qubit counts: 100-1000+ qubits
- Key challenges: Coherence, connectivity, scalability
- Hot topics: 3D integration, modular architectures, fluxonium qubits

**Trapped Ions**
- Current leaders: IonQ, Honeywell/Quantinuum, AQT
- Qubit counts: 20-50 fully connected
- Key challenges: Speed, scaling, photonic interconnects
- Hot topics: QCCD, integrated photonics, metastable qubits

**Neutral Atoms**
- Current leaders: QuEra, Pasqal, Atom Computing
- Qubit counts: 100-1000+ atoms
- Key challenges: Mid-circuit measurement, atom retention
- Hot topics: Rydberg arrays, dual-species systems, error correction

**Photonics**
- Current leaders: Xanadu, PsiQuantum
- Key challenges: Deterministic gates, loss
- Hot topics: GKP encoding, fusion-based QC, integrated photonics

**Topological**
- Current leaders: Microsoft
- Status: Early demonstrations (2025 Majorana chip)
- Key challenges: Verifying topology, braiding
- Hot topics: Hybrid systems, topological codes

#### Quantum Error Correction

- Surface code: Most developed, high threshold but large overhead
- QLDPC codes: Lower overhead, active research area
- Bosonic codes: GKP, cat qubits for continuous variables
- Color codes: Transversal gates, topological protection

#### Algorithms and Applications

- Fault-tolerant algorithms: Focus on practical speedups
- NISQ applications: Chemistry, optimization, ML
- Quantum simulation: Many-body physics, materials
- Quantum ML: Uncertain advantage, active exploration

## Part 3: Research Question Development

### 3.1 From Area to Question

Good research questions are:
- **Specific:** Not "improve quantum computing" but "reduce T-gate overhead in surface codes"
- **Answerable:** Can be addressed in 2-3 years with available resources
- **Significant:** The answer matters to the field
- **Novel:** Not already solved
