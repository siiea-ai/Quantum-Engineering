## Overview
**Day 468** | Year 1, Month 17, Week 67 | Quantum Reflection

## Core Content

### Classical vs Quantum

- Classical: Particle with E > V always passes (T = 1)
- Quantum: Partial reflection even above barrier!

### WKB Above-Barrier Reflection

For smooth potential variation:
$$R \approx \exp\left(-2\text{Im}\int\sqrt{2m(E-V)}\,dx\right)$$

When V varies slowly, reflection is exponentially small.

### Sharp Potential Step

Not WKB regime, but illustrative:
$$R = \left(\frac{k_1 - k_2}{k_1 + k_2}\right)^2$$

where k₁ = √(2mE)/ℏ and k₂ = √(2m(E-V₀))/ℏ.

### Applications

- Cold atoms reflecting from surfaces
- Neutron optics
- Quantum reflection from van der Waals potentials
