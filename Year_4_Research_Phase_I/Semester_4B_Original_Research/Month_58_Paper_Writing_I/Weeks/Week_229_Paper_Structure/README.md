## Key Concepts

### The Hourglass Model

Scientific papers follow an hourglass shape:

```
BROAD    → Introduction: General context
           ↓
           Problem statement
           ↓
NARROW   → Methods: Specific approach
           ↓
           Results: Specific findings
           ↓
           Discussion: Interpretation
           ↓
BROAD    → Conclusions: General implications
```

### Section Purposes

| Section | Purpose | Reader Question Answered |
|---------|---------|-------------------------|
| Abstract | Complete summary | "Should I read this?" |
| Introduction | Context & motivation | "Why does this matter?" |
| Methods | Technical approach | "How did they do it?" |
| Results | Findings | "What did they find?" |
| Discussion | Interpretation | "What does it mean?" |
| Conclusions | Summary & outlook | "What's next?" |

### The IMRAD Structure

Most physics papers follow IMRAD (Introduction, Methods, Results, And Discussion):

1. **Introduction:** Establishes the problem and your approach
2. **Methods:** Provides reproducibility information
3. **Results:** Presents findings without interpretation
4. **Discussion:** Interprets findings in context

Variations include:
- Combined Results & Discussion
- Theory section (before Methods in theoretical papers)
- Separate Conclusions section

## Journal Selection Criteria

### Primary Factors

1. **Scope Alignment:** Does the journal publish your type of work?
2. **Audience Match:** Will your target readers see it here?
3. **Impact Factor:** Appropriate prestige level for your work
4. **Turnaround Time:** Can you meet degree timeline?
5. **Open Access Options:** Funding for publication fees?

### Secondary Factors

1. **Review Quality:** Are reviews constructive?
2. **Acceptance Rate:** Realistic chance of acceptance?
3. **Formatting Requirements:** Compatible with your skills?
4. **Reputation in Subfield:** What do colleagues think?

## Common Challenges

### "I don't know which journal to target"

Start with where similar papers in your field appear. Ask your advisor and look at the reference lists in papers closest to yours. Aim for the most prestigious journal where your work has a reasonable chance.

### "My outline keeps changing"

This is normal at this stage. The outline is a thinking tool, not a contract. Allow flexibility while maintaining the core structure. Major outline changes become harder once writing begins.

### "I'm not sure what my main contribution is"

Try completing this sentence: "Before this work, we knew X. After this work, we now know Y." The Y is your contribution. If you can't articulate it, your work may not be ready for publication, or you need to think more deeply about significance.

## Connection to Paper Quality

The time invested this week directly impacts your paper's quality:

- **Good outline** = Logical flow and complete argumentation
- **Right journal** = Appropriate audience and review standards
- **Clear story** = Engaging paper that gets cited
- **Figure strategy** = Efficient writing and clear presentation

Rushing this phase leads to structural revisions later, which are much more time-consuming than content revisions.
