## Part 2: Risk Assessment

### 2.1 Probability-Impact Definitions

**Probability Scale:**

| Level | Probability | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| 5 | Very High (>80%) | Almost certain to occur |
| 4 | High (60-80%) | More likely than not |
| 3 | Medium (40-60%) | Could go either way |
| 2 | Low (20-40%) | Unlikely but possible |
| 1 | Very Low (<20%) | Remote possibility |

**Impact Scale:**

| Level | Impact | Schedule Effect | Scope Effect | Quality Effect |
|-------|--------|-----------------|--------------|----------------|
| 5 | Catastrophic | >3 months delay | Major deliverable lost | PhD not completable |
| 4 | Major | 1-3 months delay | Significant scope reduction | Major rework needed |
| 3 | Moderate | 2-4 weeks delay | Some scope reduction | Noticeable quality impact |
| 2 | Minor | 1-2 weeks delay | Minor scope adjustment | Small quality impact |
| 1 | Negligible | <1 week delay | No scope change | Minimal quality impact |

### 2.2 Risk Assessment Matrix

**Probability-Impact Grid:**

```
            │ Impact →
            │  1    2    3    4    5
Probability │ Neg  Minor Mod Major Cata
─────────────┼──────────────────────────
   5 V.High │  5   10   15   20   25
   4 High   │  4    8   12   16   20
   3 Medium │  3    6    9   12   15
   2 Low    │  2    4    6    8   10
   1 V.Low  │  1    2    3    4    5

Risk Score = Probability × Impact

Priority Zones:
  1-4:   Low priority (accept/monitor)
  5-9:   Medium priority (mitigation planned)
  10-15: High priority (active mitigation)
  16-25: Critical (immediate action required)
```

## Part 3: Risk Response Planning

### 3.1 Response Strategy Options

| Strategy | Description | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Avoid** | Change plan to eliminate risk | High impact, avoidable risks |
| **Mitigate** | Reduce probability or impact | Significant risks worth addressing |
| **Transfer** | Shift risk to another party | Risks others can better manage |
| **Accept** | Acknowledge and monitor | Low priority or unavoidable risks |
| **Contingency** | Plan response if risk occurs | Unmitigatable but manageable risks |

## Part 4: Quantum Engineering Specific Risks

### 4.1 Common Quantum Research Risks

| Risk Category | Specific Risk | Mitigation Approaches |
|---------------|--------------|----------------------|
| **Decoherence** | Coherence times insufficient for experiments | Improved shielding, pulse sequences, sample selection |
| **Fabrication** | Low device yield | Multiple fabrication runs, backup designs |
| **Calibration** | Calibration drift during experiments | Regular recalibration, automated monitoring |
| **Cryogenics** | Cryostat failure or limited access | Scheduled maintenance, backup time slots |
| **Lasers/Optics** | Alignment drift, mode instability | Regular alignment checks, environmental control |
| **Software** | Quantum SDK bugs or limitations | Version control, fallback implementations |
| **Simulation** | Classical simulation limits reached | Algorithm optimization, cloud resources |
| **Reproducibility** | Results not reproducible | Detailed protocols, multiple trials |
