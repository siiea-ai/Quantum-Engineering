**Focus:** Conducting small-scale experiments to test assumptions and refine approaches

Week 215 transitions from planning to action through pilot studies. ## Key Concepts

### What is a Pilot Study?

A pilot study is a small-scale preliminary investigation conducted to:
- **Test feasibility** of proposed methods
- **Validate assumptions** underlying the research plan
- **Identify problems** before they become costly
- **Estimate parameters** (effect sizes, variability, timing)
- **Train researchers** on procedures and equipment

### Pilot Studies vs. Exploratory Research

| Aspect | Pilot Study | Exploratory Research |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| Goal | Validate specific assumptions | Discover patterns or phenomena |
| Scope | Narrow, focused | Broad, open-ended |
| Analysis | Confirmatory orientation | Hypothesis-generating |
| Sample size | Minimal for feasibility | Variable |
| Documentation | Process-focused | Finding-focused |

### Types of Pilot Studies

**Feasibility Pilots:**
- Can the proposed method be executed?
- Are resources sufficient?
- Is the timeline realistic?

**Measurement Pilots:**
- Do instruments work as expected?
- What is the measurement variability?
- Are calibrations stable?

**Protocol Pilots:**
- Are procedures clear and repeatable?
- What are common failure modes?
- How long do procedures actually take?

**Analysis Pilots:**
- Do analysis methods work with realistic data?
- Are computational resources sufficient?
- What are preliminary effect sizes?

### What Pilots Cannot Do

Pilot studies have important limitations:
- **Not for hypothesis testing:** Sample sizes too small for statistical power
- **Not generalizable:** Results may not scale to full study
- **Not definitive:** Negative results don't prove method won't work
- **Not publishable (usually):** Insufficient rigor for formal publication

## Quantum Engineering Pilot Study Examples

### Example 1: Qubit Coherence Measurement Pilot

**Assumption to Test:** T2 measurements can be completed within 2 hours per sample

**Pilot Design:**
- Measure T2 on 3 samples from different batches
- Track total time including setup, calibration, measurement, analysis
- Document any unexpected delays or issues

**Success Criteria:**
- Average total time per sample < 2.5 hours
- Measurement protocol completed without major issues
- T2 values within expected range (validate measurement setup)

### Example 2: Quantum Algorithm Implementation Pilot

**Assumption to Test:** VQE implementation can converge for target molecules

**Pilot Design:**
- Implement VQE for simple test case (H2 at equilibrium)
- Run on local simulator and cloud quantum hardware
- Track convergence behavior and computational resources

**Success Criteria:**
- Achieves known ground state energy within chemical accuracy
- Convergence within 100 iterations
- No critical software errors

### Example 3: Sample Preparation Pilot

**Assumption to Test:** Diamond surface preparation achieves required NV center density

**Pilot Design:**
- Prepare 5 diamond samples using proposed protocol
- Characterize NV density using confocal microscopy
- Measure T2 of selected NV centers

**Success Criteria:**
- NV density within 1-10 NV/um^2 in 4/5 samples
- At least 3 samples have NV centers with T2 > 100 us
- Protocol completed within 1 week

## Common Pitfalls

### Over-Interpreting Results
- **Problem:** Drawing strong conclusions from small samples
- **Solution:** Focus on feasibility, not statistical significance

### Under-Documenting
- **Problem:** Rushing execution without recording details
- **Solution:** Document everything, including "obvious" observations

### Scope Creep
- **Problem:** Pilot expands into full study
- **Solution:** Define strict boundaries before starting

### Confirmation Bias
- **Problem:** Seeing only evidence that supports assumptions
- **Solution:** Actively seek disconfirming evidence

### Ignoring Negative Results
- **Problem:** Dismissing problems as "pilot issues"
- **Solution:** Take all results seriously, investigate problems
