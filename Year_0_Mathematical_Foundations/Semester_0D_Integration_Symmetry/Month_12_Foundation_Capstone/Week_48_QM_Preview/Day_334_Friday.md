## 1. The Measurement Problem

### The Core Issue

Before measurement: $|\psi\rangle = \sum_n c_n |n\rangle$ (superposition)

After measurement: $|n\rangle$ (definite state)

**When and how does this "collapse" happen?**

### Schrödinger's Cat

A cat in superposition of alive and dead?
Illustrates the absurdity of macroscopic superposition.

---

## 2. Major Interpretations

### Copenhagen (Standard)

- Wavefunction: Tool for predicting probabilities
- Collapse: Real, instantaneous, upon "observation"
- Reality: Only defined relative to measurement

### Many-Worlds (Everett)

- No collapse: Universe branches at each measurement
- All outcomes occur in different branches
- Reality: Many parallel worlds

### Pilot Wave (Bohm)

- Particles have definite positions always
- Wavefunction guides particle motion
- Deterministic, but non-local

### Consistent Histories

- Focus on consistent sets of histories
- Probability assigned to histories
- No single "true" sequence of events

### QBism (Quantum Bayesianism)

- Wavefunction: Agent's belief state
- Probabilities: Subjective, not objective
- Measurement: Update of beliefs

---

## 3. What We Can Say

### What QM Predicts (Unambiguous)

- Probabilities of measurement outcomes
- Evolution of wavefunctions
- Correlations in entangled systems

### What QM Doesn't Tell Us

- What happens between measurements
- Why particular outcomes occur
- The "true nature" of reality

---

## 4. The Pragmatic Approach

### "Shut Up and Calculate"

For practical purposes:
1. Apply the postulates
2. Compute predictions
3. Compare with experiment

### The Success of QM

- Predicts all atomic and subatomic phenomena
- Agrees with experiment to 12 decimal places (QED)
- Foundation of modern technology
