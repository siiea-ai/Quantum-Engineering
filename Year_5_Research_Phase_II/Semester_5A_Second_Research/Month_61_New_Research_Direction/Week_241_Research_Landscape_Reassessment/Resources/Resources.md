# Week 241 Resources: Research Landscape Reassessment

## Primary Literature Databases

### arXiv.org
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/

The primary preprint server for physics research. Essential for staying current with developments before peer review.

**Key Categories for Quantum Engineering:**
- **quant-ph**: Quantum Physics - core quantum information and computation
- **cond-mat.mes-hall**: Mesoscale and Nanoscale Physics - solid-state qubits, nanofabrication
- **cond-mat.supr-con**: Superconductivity - superconducting qubits, materials
- **cond-mat.str-el**: Strongly Correlated Electrons - many-body physics, topological phases
- **physics.atom-ph**: Atomic Physics - trapped ions, neutral atoms, AMO
- **physics.optics**: Optics - photonic qubits, quantum optics
- **cs.ET**: Emerging Technologies - quantum computing algorithms, applications

**Advanced Search Tips:**
- Use `au:LastName` for author searches
- Use `ti:keyword` for title searches
- Combine with date ranges: `submittedDate:[202401 TO 202602]`
- Use `abs:keyword` for abstract searches

**RSS Feeds:**
Set up daily feeds for your key categories to stay current.

---

### Google Scholar
**URL:** https://scholar.google.com/

Comprehensive academic search including citations, related work, and author profiles.

**Features for Landscape Analysis:**
- **Cited by**: Find papers citing key works
- **Related articles**: Discover connected research
- **Author profiles**: Track researcher output
- **Alerts**: Set up keyword and author notifications
- **Metrics**: Journal and conference rankings

**Search Strategies:**
- Use quotes for exact phrases: "quantum error correction"
- Use OR for alternatives: superconducting OR transmon
- Use - to exclude: quantum -chemistry
- Use site: for specific sources: site:nature.com

---

**URL:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/

### Connected Papers
**URL:** https://www.connectedpapers.com/

Visual literature mapping tool for exploring paper relationships.

**How to Use:**
1. Enter a key paper's DOI or title
2. View graph of related papers
3. Identify clusters of related work
4. Find foundational (prior) works
5. Find derivative (subsequent) works

**Best Practices:**
- Start with highly-cited review papers
- Look for isolated clusters (potential gaps)
- Identify bridges between clusters (interdisciplinary opportunities)

## Emerging Topics to Watch (2025-2026)

*These topics may have significant developments since your Year 4 research*

1. **Logical qubit demonstrations** - Multiple platforms showing error-corrected logical qubits
2. **Quantum error correction thresholds** - Experimental approaches to fault-tolerance
3. **Neutral atom quantum computing** - Rapid scaling and improved fidelities
4. **Hybrid classical-quantum algorithms** - Practical applications of near-term devices
5. **Quantum machine learning** - Theoretical understanding and practical demonstrations
6. **Bosonic codes** - Cat qubits, GKP states, and other continuous variable approaches
7. **Topological qubits** - Progress toward non-Abelian anyons
8. **Quantum networking** - Metropolitan and longer-range quantum networks
9. **Quantum sensing applications** - Medical, geological, navigation applications
10. **Quantum simulation** - Programmable analog and digital quantum simulators
