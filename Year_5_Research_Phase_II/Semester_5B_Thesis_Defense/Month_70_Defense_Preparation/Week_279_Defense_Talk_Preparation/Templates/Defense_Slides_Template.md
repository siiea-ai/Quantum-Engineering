# Defense Slides Template

## Overview

This template provides the structure and content guidelines for your 45-minute defense presentation. Use this as a planning document before creating your actual slides.

---

## Presentation Specifications

| Specification | Requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| Total Duration | 45 minutes |
| Total Slides | 40-50 main slides |
| Backup Slides | 10-15 additional |
| Format | 16:9 aspect ratio preferred |
| Font (titles) | 36-44 pt |
| Font (body) | 24-28 pt |
| Font (minimum) | 18 pt |

---

### Slide 2: Opening Hook

**Time:** 1 minute

**Purpose:** Capture attention, establish significance

**Content Prompt:**
- What is the big picture motivation?
- Why should anyone care about this research?
- What is the compelling story that brings us here?

**Example Approaches:**
1. Start with a striking fact or statistic
2. Begin with a fundamental question
3. Show a compelling image that captures the essence
4. Connect to broader societal/technological impact

**Speaker Notes Template:**
```
Opening line: [Write a compelling opening sentence]

Main hook: [2-3 sentences establishing importance]

Transition: "To address this challenge, my research has focused on..."
```

---

### Slides 3-4: Field Context

**Time:** 2 minutes (1 min each)

**Purpose:** Establish necessary background

**Slide 3 Content:**
- Current state of the field
- Key prior achievements
- Important concepts audience needs

**Slide 4 Content:**
- More specific context for your work
- Relevant theoretical framework
- Key experimental/computational advances

**Speaker Notes Template:**
```
Slide 3: "The field of [X] has made remarkable progress in [achievements].
Key developments include [A], [B], and [C]..."

Slide 4: "More specifically, my work builds on the foundations of [specific
background]. Understanding [concept] is essential because..."
```

---

### Slides 5-6: The Gap/Problem

**Time:** 2 minutes (1 min each)

**Purpose:** Define what was unknown/unsolved

**Slide 5 Content:**
- What was the open question?
- What limitation existed?
- Why was this important to address?

**Slide 6 Content:**
- Specific challenges
- Why prior approaches were insufficient
- What made this hard?

**Speaker Notes Template:**
```
Slide 5: "However, a key challenge remained: [state the gap]. Despite
[prior work], we still did not understand [unknown]..."

Slide 6: "This problem was particularly challenging because [reasons].
Previous attempts using [approach] were limited by [limitation]..."
```

---

### Slides 7-8: Research Questions & Approach

**Time:** 2.5 minutes

**Purpose:** State your contribution focus

**Slide 7 Content - Research Questions:**
- 2-3 specific research questions
- Clear, answerable formulations
- Connect to the gap identified

**Slide 8 Content - Approach Overview:**
- Your general strategy
- Key innovations
- Brief methodology preview

**Speaker Notes Template:**
```
Slide 7: "My thesis addresses three central questions:
1. [Question 1]
2. [Question 2]
3. [Question 3]"

Slide 8: "To answer these questions, I developed [approach], which enabled
[capability]. This approach combines [methods]..."
```

---

### Slides 9-10: Outline/Preview

**Time:** 2 minutes

**Purpose:** Roadmap for the presentation

**Slide 9 Content:**
- Visual outline of presentation structure
- Key sections highlighted
- Time indicators (optional)

**Slide 10 Content:**
- Preview of main contributions
- What audience will learn
- Transition to first research section

**Speaker Notes Template:**
```
Slide 9: "Here's how I'll present this work today. First, I'll discuss
[Project 1], then move to [Project 2]. I'll conclude with synthesis and
future directions. Total time: 45 minutes, then questions."

Slide 10: "By the end of this talk, you'll see how we [contribution 1],
[contribution 2], and [contribution 3]. Let's begin with..."
```

## Design Guidelines

### Color Scheme

**Recommended Palette:**
```
Primary (titles, emphasis):     #2C3E50 (dark blue-gray)
Secondary (accents):            #3498DB (bright blue)
Background:                     #FFFFFF (white) or #F5F5F5 (light gray)
Text:                          #333333 (dark gray)
Highlight:                     #E74C3C (red) or #27AE60 (green)
```

### Typography

**Font Recommendations:**
- Titles: Sans-serif (Helvetica, Arial, Calibri) - Bold
- Body: Sans-serif (same family) - Regular
- Code/Data: Monospace (Consolas, Courier)

**Size Guidelines:**
- Slide titles: 36-44 pt
- Section headers: 28-32 pt
- Body text: 24-28 pt
- Caption text: 18-22 pt
- Fine print: 16-18 pt (use sparingly)

### Figure Guidelines

- Resolution: 300 dpi minimum
- Format: PNG or PDF for best quality
- Size: Fill available space appropriately
- Labels: Match presentation font family
- Axis text: Minimum 18 pt in final slide
