## Formatting Guidelines

### Equations
- Display equations: `$$...$$` or `\begin{equation}...\end{equation}`
- Key results: `$$\boxed{...}$$`
- Numbered for reference: `\begin{equation}\label{eq:name}...\end{equation}`

### Figures
- High resolution (300 DPI)
- Consistent style with R1 chapters
- Complete captions
- Referenced in text before appearing

### Tables
- Consistent formatting
- Descriptive captions
- Units clearly indicated
- Source noted if from literature

### Citations
- Consistent style (APS, IEEE, or department standard)
- Complete bibliographic information
- DOIs included

## Template Usage Notes

1. **Expand, don't copy**: Use your paper as a starting point, but rewrite for thesis format
2. **Add derivations**: Include all mathematical steps skipped in the paper
3. **Explain choices**: Discuss why you made methodological decisions
4. **Connect to R1**: Maintain explicit links to your first research project
5. **Be self-contained**: Reader should not need to consult the paper
