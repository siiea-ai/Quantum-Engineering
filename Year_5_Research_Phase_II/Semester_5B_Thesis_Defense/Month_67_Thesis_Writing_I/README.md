## Writing Quality Standards

### Technical Accuracy
- All equations derived from first principles or properly cited
- Physical interpretations accompany mathematical statements
- Notation consistent throughout and with field conventions

### Scholarly Voice
- Precise, unambiguous language
- Appropriate use of first person ("we" for collaborative work, "I" for solo contributions)
- Active voice preferred for clarity
- Hedge appropriately but not excessively

### Structure
- Clear topic sentences for each paragraph
- Logical flow within and between sections
- Transitions that guide the reader
- Balanced coverage of subtopics

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Too Much Background**: Focus on what's relevant to your thesis
2. **Too Little Background**: Don't assume reader knows your specialized area
3. **Passive Voice Overuse**: Be direct and clear
4. **Missing Connections**: Explicitly link background to your contributions
5. **Outdated References**: Check for recent relevant publications
6. **Inconsistent Notation**: Define once, use consistently
7. **Perfectionism Early**: Get words on paper, revise later
