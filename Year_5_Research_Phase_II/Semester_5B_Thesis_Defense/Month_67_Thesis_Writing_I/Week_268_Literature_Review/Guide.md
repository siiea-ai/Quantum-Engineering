## Literature Integration Techniques

### Technique 1: Contextual Citation

Weave references naturally into claims:

**Weak:**
> "Surface codes have been studied extensively [1-15]."

**Strong:**
> "Surface codes were introduced by Kitaev [2003] and have since become
> a leading approach for fault-tolerant quantum computing, with recent
> experiments demonstrating error suppression [Google 2023] and logical
> operations [IBM 2024]."

### Technique 2: Comparative Citation

Show how your work relates to others:

> "While prior approaches to noise-adapted codes focused on threshold
> optimization [Tuckett 2018, 2020], our work addresses the
> complementary question of practical implementation overhead [cf.
> Gidney 2021]."

### Technique 3: Foundational Citation

Cite foundational work for established concepts:

> "Quantum error correction was introduced by Shor [1995] and Steane
> [1996], with the stabilizer formalism developed by Gottesman [1997]."

### Technique 4: Recent Citation

Demonstrate awareness of recent progress:

> "Recent experimental advances have demonstrated surface code logical
> qubits [Acharya et al. 2024, Chen et al. 2025], bringing fault-tolerant
> quantum computing closer to practical realization."

## Common Citation Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Citation Dumping
**Wrong:** "Many researchers have studied this [1-47]."
**Right:** Cite specifically and explain relevance.

### Mistake 2: Missing Key Citations
**Wrong:** Citing only your group's papers
**Right:** Fair coverage of the field, including competitors

### Mistake 3: Outdated References
**Wrong:** Most recent citation from 2020 in a rapidly moving field
**Right:** Include 2024-2026 references where relevant

### Mistake 4: Incorrect Attribution
**Wrong:** Attributing a result to the wrong paper
**Right:** Verify primary sources for major claims

### Mistake 5: Over-Citation
**Wrong:** Citing 5 papers for an uncontroversial claim
**Right:** 1-2 authoritative sources suffice

## Reference Management

### Recommended Tools

1. **Zotero** (free, browser integration, collaboration)
2. **Mendeley** (free, good PDF management)
3. **BibDesk** (Mac, direct BibTeX editing)
4. **JabRef** (cross-platform, BibTeX-native)

### BibTeX Best Practices

```bibtex
@article{lastname2024keyword,
  author  = {LastName, FirstName and ...},
  title   = {Full Title in Sentence Case},
  journal = {Full Journal Name},
  volume  = {XXX},
  pages   = {XXX--YYY},
  year    = {2024},
  doi     = {10.XXXX/...}
}
```

Key conventions:
- Cite key format: `lastname2024keyword`
- Complete author list (use "and others" only if >6)
- Full journal names (not abbreviations)
- Always include DOI when available
- Include arXiv ID for preprints
