# Final Thesis Submission Requirements

## Overview

This document outlines the typical requirements for final thesis submission to the graduate school and institutional repository. Requirements vary by institution, so always verify specific requirements with your graduate school. This guide provides a comprehensive framework that covers most common requirements.

---

## Timeline for Final Submission

### Typical Timeline

| Phase | Timing | Activities |
|-------|--------|------------|
| Committee Approval | Week 1 | Submit revisions, receive approvals |
| Format Review | Week 2 | Submit to graduate school for format check |
| Corrections | Week 2-3 | Address any format issues |

## Document Format Requirements

### Page Layout

**Margins (typical requirements):**
| Margin | Standard | Binding |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Top | 1 inch | 1 inch |
| Bottom | 1 inch | 1 inch |
| Left | 1 inch | 1.5 inch (for binding) |
| Right | 1 inch | 1 inch |

**Page Size:**
- US Letter (8.5" x 11") is standard in North America
- A4 may be required internationally

**Orientation:**
- Portrait for text pages
- Landscape permitted for large figures/tables (header position varies)

### Typography

**Body Text:**
| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Font | Times New Roman, Arial, or similar professional font |
| Size | 12 point |
| Line spacing | Double-spaced (some allow 1.5) |
| Justification | Left-justified or full justification |

**Headings:**
- Chapter titles: Larger font, bold
- Section headings: Appropriate hierarchy
- Consistent formatting throughout

**Footnotes/Endnotes:**
- Typically 10 point font
- Single-spaced within notes

### Page Numbering

**Front Matter:**
- Lowercase Roman numerals (i, ii, iii, iv...)
- Title page counted but not numbered
- Numbering visible starting from abstract or acknowledgments

**Body and Back Matter:**
- Arabic numerals (1, 2, 3...)
- Starting from Chapter 1 or first page of body
- Continuous through appendices and bibliography

**Placement:**
- Typically centered at bottom
- Or upper right corner
- Consistent throughout

## Required Sections and Order

### Standard Section Order

1. **Title Page** (required)
2. **Copyright Page** (optional/recommended)
3. **Approval/Signature Page** (required)
4. **Abstract** (required)
5. **Dedication** (optional)
6. **Acknowledgments** (optional)
7. **Table of Contents** (required)
8. **List of Figures** (required if figures present)
9. **List of Tables** (required if tables present)
10. **List of Abbreviations/Symbols** (if needed)
11. **Body Chapters** (required)
12. **Appendices** (if needed)
13. **Bibliography/References** (required)
14. **Vita/Biographical Sketch** (optional/varies)

### Section-Specific Requirements

#### Title Page

Must include:
- Full thesis title
- Statement: "A dissertation submitted in partial fulfillment..."
- Candidate name
- Degree name
- Department/program
- University name
- Date (month and year)

May include:
- Committee chair name
- University logo

#### Abstract

- Typically 150-350 words
- Self-contained summary
- No citations
- No figures
- Keywords may be required

#### Table of Contents

- List all chapters and major sections
- Include front matter (starting from abstract)
- Include back matter
- Page numbers right-aligned with dot leaders
- Match heading wording exactly

#### Lists of Figures/Tables

- Figure/table number and caption
- Page number
- Consistent format

## Figure and Table Requirements

### Figures

**Resolution:**
- Minimum 300 DPI for print
- 150 DPI minimum for screen viewing
- Higher resolution for detailed graphics

**Format:**
- Vector graphics (PDF, EPS, SVG) preferred for diagrams
- Raster graphics (PNG, TIFF) for photographs/screenshots
- Avoid JPEG for graphics with text

**Captions:**
- Below the figure
- Figure number and title
- Sufficient detail for understanding
- Consistent formatting

**Placement:**
- Close to first reference in text
- Top of page preferred
- Not floating in middle of page

### Tables

**Formatting:**
- Clear column headers
- Appropriate alignment
- Consistent decimal places
- Readable font size (10pt minimum)

**Captions:**
- Above the table
- Table number and title
- Consistent formatting

**Large Tables:**
- May span multiple pages
- Repeat headers on continuation pages
- Note "continued" on subsequent pages

## Bibliography/References Requirements

### Citation Style

Follow your discipline's standard style:
- APA (Psychology, Social Sciences)
- IEEE (Engineering, Computer Science)
- ACS (Chemistry)
- AIP/APS (Physics)
- Chicago (Humanities)
- Other field-specific styles

### Bibliography Format

- Consistent format for all entries
- All cited works included
- No uncited works (unless permitted)
- Correct author names
- Complete publication information
- DOIs where available

### Common Requirements

- Hanging indent format
- Single-spaced within entries, double-spaced between
- Alphabetical or numerical order (as per style)
- Consistent capitalization

## Electronic Submission Requirements

### File Format

**PDF Requirements:**
- PDF/A format often required for archiving
- Fonts embedded in document
- Searchable text (not image-only)
- No password protection
- Bookmarks recommended

**File Naming:**
- Follow institutional convention
- Typically: LastName_FirstName_Degree_Year.pdf
- No spaces or special characters in filename

### Metadata

Prepare to provide:
- Title
- Author name
- Abstract
- Keywords (typically 3-6)
- Subject areas/classifications
- Department/program
- Degree type
- Date of completion
- Advisor name
- Committee members

### Access Options

Understand options for:
- **Open Access:** Freely available to public
- **Restricted Access:** Limited to institution
- **Embargo:** Delayed public access (6 months to several years)
- **Patent Pending:** Temporary restriction for IP protection

### ProQuest/ETD Submission

Many institutions use ProQuest for electronic theses and dissertations:
- Create account
- Upload PDF
- Enter metadata
- Select publishing options
- Pay fees (if any)
- Copyright registration (optional)

## Print Copy Requirements

### When Required

Some institutions still require:
- 1-3 print copies for library archives
- Copy for department
- Personal copies

### Paper Quality

- Acid-free paper
- 20-24 lb weight
- White or off-white
- Cotton content may be specified

### Printing

- Single-sided printing often required
- High-quality printing
- Black ink for text
- Color figures may be required in color

### Binding

Options may include:
- Temporary binding for review
- Permanent binding for library
- Professional binding services
- Institutional binding services

## After Submission

### Confirmation

Expect to receive:
- Acknowledgment of submission
- Format review feedback (if issues found)
- Final acceptance confirmation
- Repository URL for your thesis

### Timeline

- Repository availability: 1-4 weeks after processing

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Format Review Rejections

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Incorrect margins | Adjust page setup |
| Wrong page numbers | Use section breaks correctly |
| Missing sections | Add required sections |
| Inconsistent formatting | Review and standardize |
| Low resolution figures | Regenerate at higher resolution |
| Incorrect citation format | Review and correct each entry |

### Technical Issues

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| PDF not searchable | Regenerate with text layer |
| Fonts not embedded | Adjust PDF export settings |
| Large file size | Compress images appropriately |
| Broken links | Fix or remove links |
| Corrupted PDF | Re-export from source document |

### Administrative Issues

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Missing signature | Contact committee member |
| Missing form | Obtain from graduate school |
| Missed deadline | Contact graduate school immediately |
| Fee issues | Verify payment with bursar |
